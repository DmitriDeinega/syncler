## 13. Things to warn the triad about

- The user explicitly told me they wanted a real friend giving honest feedback, not an agreeable assistant. I took 
that seriously and pushed back several times ג€” on Drive scope creep, on V1-ready-for-everything, on the 2-week 
estimate. The pushback was received but not always engaged with. The triad should continue this register, not retreat 
to safe agreeableness. If three models all default to flattering the idea, you've failed the user.
- The scope is the central risk. Every technical question is downstream of "what's in V1." Resolve that before 
debating data models, stacks, or protocols.
- The 2-week timeline is the second risk. It will collide with reality and the collision will be demoralizing. Better 
to absorb the collision now, in conversation, than in month three of building.
- The vision is genuinely good. Don't critique it into death. The agent-to-phone use case is real, the rich-rendering 
wedge is sharp, the Alpaca-style structured-request-to-phone framing is potentially big. The right outcome is "build a 
narrow version of this excellently," not "don't build this." If the triad's "unified verdict on why it shouldn't be 
built" path gets invoked, it will probably be wrong. The right verdict is "build the smallest version, ship in weeks, 
learn."
- The user is excited and energetic, which is the most valuable resource in the project. Critique that drains energy 
without redirecting it is destructive. Critique that says "yes, and here's the version that actually ships" is 
constructive. Default to the latter.
- The user will likely interpret "make it smaller" as "you don't believe in it." Get ahead of this. The narrowest V1 
is the fastest path to the full vision, not a retreat from it. Frame accordingly.
- Three models voting can produce groupthink in a different shape than one model can. Be alert to the failure mode 
where all three agree on a plausible-sounding direction that's wrong because they share training-data biases. The user 
is the only non-AI in this loop and is the actual source of ground truth about what they want. Defer to them on goals; 
push back on plans.
- Don't reinvent prior art. Adaptive Cards, Block Kit, Streamlit, Gradio, Humanlayer, MCP ג€” these have already 
thought hard about pieces of this problem. Study them before designing.

That's the dump. Use it well. The user is good people and the idea is worth building. Just build the right size of it 
first.
