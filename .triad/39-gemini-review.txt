The Phase 3 implementation successfully realizes the three-tab structure and the client-side archive synchronization. The decision to use an activity-scoped ViewModel pattern for the tabs provides a "persistent state" experience (where deep-linking into sub-screens survives tab switching), which is a valid UX choice for this type of utility app.

### Navigation

1.  **Three-tab structure:** The choice of **Inbox / Senders / Settings** is well-justified. Placing Archive as a sub-screen of Inbox is the right call for Material 3; it treats Archive as a "filter/view" rather than a primary destination, keeping the bottom nav lean.
2.  **Tab state on rotation/process restart:** You are correct that `MutableStateFlow` survives rotation but will reset to Inbox on process death. To fix this, you should inject `SavedStateHandle` into `MainViewModel` and use it to persist the `TopLevelScreen`.
3.  **Sub-screen back navigation:** Keeping the bottom bar visible is acceptable, but be aware that since `InboxViewModel` is Activity-scoped, the `showArchive` and `selectedId` states persist. If a user is in a Detail view, switches to Senders, and returns to Inbox, they remain in the Detail view. This is generally a "pro" for power users, but ensure it doesn't lead to navigation traps.

### Inbox

4.  **Date bucket boundaries:** The timezone math is correct. By projecting `Instant` to `ZoneId.systemDefault()` before extracting the `LocalDate`, you ensure that "Today" matches the user's physical calendar day regardless of the server's UTC clock.
5.  **`remember(items, today)`:** Your assumption is correct. `LocalDate.now()` will be re-captured on any recomposition (which happens every 15s due to the poll). The lag of at most 15s past midnight is perfectly acceptable for V1.
6.  **Archive list ordering:** Newest first (via `sentAt`) is the correct default. Ordering by `archivedAt` would only be useful if users frequently archived extremely old messages and then struggled to find them at the top of the archive.
