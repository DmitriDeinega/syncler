# Consultation 102: Review of Phase 8a spec v4

## Executive Summary: Dual GREEN

The v4 specification successfully addresses the edge cases and race conditions identified in previous rounds. The shift to a coarse-grained user-row lock (`SELECT ... FOR UPDATE`) across all state-mutating endpoints provides a robust foundation for serializing rotations against concurrent updates, while the granular CAS checks ensure client-server synchronization.

---

## 1. Per-Section Assessment

### §10.5: User-Row Lock Synchronization — **GREEN**
The expanded "MUST" clause is the most critical addition. By listing specific endpoints, you eliminate ambiguity for implementers. 
*   **Completeness:** The list correctly identifies all current mutations of `key_generation`-tagged data. 
*   **Logic:** Taking the lock as the *first* DB operation ensures that the `key_generation` observed by any mutation is stable until the transaction completes. This effectively serializes the entire user state tree during sensitive operations.

### §10.8: Rotation Handler Flow — **GREEN**
The rewrite is precise and follows a high-integrity security pattern.
*   **Step 2 (Locking):** Correctly positioned to prevent races between concurrent rotation attempts or between rotation and pairing.
*   **Step 4 (Failed-Proof Counter):** The explicit requirement for a separate transaction/connection for the counter is a high-signal observation. This prevents "free" brute-force attempts where a rollback would otherwise wipe the failure record. This matches best practices for `rate_limit_events` management.
*   **Steps 8 & 9 (CAS Logic):** Providing both `UPDATE ... WHERE` and `SELECT FOR UPDATE` options is helpful. Both are semantically equivalent under the existing user-row lock, but the `UPDATE ... WHERE` form is generally more efficient in Postgres.
*   **Step 12 (Session Revocation):** Crucial for `root_compromise_rotation`.
*   **Step 13 (Challenge Consumption):** Moving the `DELETE` to the very end (post-verification, pre-commit) ensures the challenge is only burned on successful state transition, which is user-friendly while remaining secure due to the Step 4 rate-limiting.

## 2. Missing / Refinements
No major components are missing. The specification is now implementation-ready.

## 3. Observations / New Notes
*   **Deadlock Risk:** Since all endpoints follow the same locking order (Users → [Blob Tables]), the risk of cross-table deadlocks is minimized. Implementers must ensure they never take a blob-level lock *before* the user-level lock.
*   **Audit Trail:** The inclusion of `paired_count` in the audit log is a smart touch for post-hoc verification of rotation integrity.

**Conclusion:** The specification is ready for inclusion in `docs/crypto-spec.md §10`.
