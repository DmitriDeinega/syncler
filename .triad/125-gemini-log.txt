3. **Answers to open questions:**
   - **A (Directory version):** Use a monotonic **integer counter** (e.g., `BIGINT`) on the `users` table. It is simpler to reason about than timestamps and avoids issues with clock skew or sub-millisecond collisions.
   - **B (Rotation cadence):** **User-triggered/Manual** is correct for V0.1. Automated rotation introduces complex state management for "grace periods" of old keys that we should avoid until the base implementation is stable.
   - **C (Android Library):** **Tink.** While BouncyCastle is present, Tink’s HPKE implementation is much more ergonomic and less prone to "assembly errors" (e.g., getting the KDF/KEM wiring wrong).
   - **D (Backfill):** **Delete historical cards.** Since this is V0.1, we should prioritize code cleanliness over data persistence. Forcing the Android client to support two different decryption paths (V1-pairing-key vs V2-HPKE) significantly increases the attack surface and complexity.
