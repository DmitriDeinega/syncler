### Findings

#### 1. Lock discipline: **GREEN**
- The `lifecycleLock` monitor is correctly used to serialize all state transitions (`start`, `stop`, `openStreamLocked`, `scheduleReconnectLocked`) and mutating listener callbacks.
- **No suspend points** are held inside the `synchronized` blocks. Specifically, `delay()` in the reconnect coroutine happens outside the lock, and the actual SSE handshake (`newEventSource`) is non-blocking.
- **External calls** (`authFailureHandler.onAuthFailure`, `_streamReady.tryEmit`) are executed after releasing the lock, preventing potential deadlocks or re-entrancy issues.

#### 2. Listener-vs-lifecycle interleaving: **GREEN**
- The identity check (`current !== eventSource`) inside the `synchronized` block for every callback (`onOpen`, `onClosed`, `onFailure`) ensures that stale events from replaced or stopped streams are ignored.
- The race where `stop()` could interleave with a mid-flight `openStreamLocked()` is closed because both now contend for the same monitor. If `stop()` runs first, `openStreamLocked` sees `wantRunning == false` and no-ops. If `openStreamLocked` runs first, `stop()` immediately cancels the newly created `current` source.

#### 3. 401 fallthrough behavior: **GREEN**
- The removal of the 401 short-circuit ensures that a stale 401 (from an old session) doesn't strand the current live session.
- The "Real 401" case is handled gracefully: `onAuthFailure` triggers a logout (clearing the token), and the subsequent reconnect attempt (after 1s) sees the null token and stops the retry loop.
- The "Infinite loop" risk is mitigated by the 1s minimum delay and the token-check in `AuthRepository`, limiting the impact to at most one or two redundant 401s before the session is cleared.

#### 4. `onEvent` lock-free identity check: **GREEN**
- Using a `@Volatile` field for `current` ensures that the hot path remains lock-free while maintaining correctness. Since mutating callbacks re-check identity under the lock, any race between `onEvent` and a state change results in a safe drop of the stale event.

#### 5. Miscellaneous: **GREEN**
- Exponential backoff (1s to 30s) is correctly implemented and reset on successful `onOpen`.
- `reconnectJob` cancellation in `stop()` prevents leaking retry coroutines after the manager is paused.

### Overall Verdict: **LAND PHASE 2**

The implementation is now robust, idiomatically sound, and fully addresses the identified edge cases. Phase 3a (Templates Renderer) is clear to proceed.
