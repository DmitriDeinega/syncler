1. You said “V1 perfect, ready for everything.” Name five things that are explicitly not in V1; if nothing is excluded, V1 is not a product plan.

2. Pick one V1 success metric: you personally use it daily for 30 days, 20 developers use it weekly, 100 strangers sign up, or one outside service integrates. Which one is the real target?

3. What is the first workflow you will run through this app every day before anyone else cares: Claude Code approval prompts, trading decisions, file/photo handoff, lottery project outputs, or something else?

4. Describe the first “perfect” card in concrete terms: what does the sender post, what does your phone show, what do you tap/type/upload, and what response goes back?

5. If we give this four weeks of part-time work instead of two weeks of fantasy full-time output, what gets cut first: plugins, file previews, push notifications, search/tags, mobile polish, or third-party pairing?

6. What is the rule for deciding whether a feature belongs in V1: “needed for my daily Claude Code workflow,” “needed by any developer,” “needed by future services,” or “cool infrastructure”?

7. When a pending card asks “Buy or Sell?” and you answer six hours later, who decides whether the answer is still valid: sender, server, user, or card schema?

8. What should happen if the sender process crashes after sending a card, the user submits twice from two devices, or the phone was offline for a week and receives stale prompts?

9. How does a new sender get permission to reach you: manually issued API key, QR pairing, email invite, public handle, phone number, or something else?

10. For stranger/service pairing, what prevents a fake “Alpaca” sender from requesting KYC photos from a user who trusts the brand name?

11. Are card contents end-to-end encrypted from sender to device, or can the server read them for search, previews, moderation, and debugging? Pick one for V1.

12. If a user uploads passport/KYC/photos through this product and data leaks, what is your intended posture: “we do not support regulated sensitive data yet,” “encrypted so we cannot see it,” or “we accept compliance obligations”?

13. Give three concrete interactions you believe cannot be handled by declarative components plus sandboxed HTML. If you cannot name them, why are renderer plugins in V1?

14. Are we building for a two-sided marketplace in V1, or deliberately ducking that and building a developer/self-use tool first? “Both” is not an answer for the first release.

15. If you are still working on this in month 3, what failure would make you quit: not using it yourself, technical complexity, no users, unreliable notifications, security anxiety, or boredom from too much infrastructure?
